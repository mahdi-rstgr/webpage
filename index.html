About Me

I'm a Postdoctoral Fellow at Acceleration Consortium. Prior to that, I obtained my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Toronto co-supervised by Prof. Hani Naguib and Prof. Chul Park .

My work spans mechanical engineering in solids and fluids, leveraging AI, machine learning, and data science to tackle challenges in design, materials, and physics-informed modeling.

Crack Detector Triboelectric Nanogenerator (CD-TENG)

Developed an ultra-lightweight aerogel-fibrous material with high thermal stability and boosted triboelectricity for crack detection in harsh environments.

Technologies: Material Synthesis, Porous Composite, Fibrous, Aerogel, Material Characterization (SEM, XRD, FTIR, DMA, WCA, TGA), Dielectrics

Results: Single Droplet Output: 7.9 V and 0.79 A (at 30 cm height / 70 incline). Peak Power Density: 33 mW/m under optimized parameters. Optimized Conditions: 15 cm droplet height, 50 substrate slope, and 20 M load. Primary Application: Lightweight, self-powered sensors for real-time crack detection in extreme environments.

View Details Demo

Sound Aerogel-based Sensor Triboelectric Nanogenerator (SAS-TENG)

Developed a ultra-lightweight aerogel composites for sound-driven triboelectric nanogenerator.

Technologies: Material Synthesis, Porous Composite, Sol-Gel Processing, Aerogel, Nanoparticle, Helmholtz Resonator, Self-Powered Sensing, Material Characterization (SEM, XRD, FTIR, DMA, WCA, TGA)

Results: Peak Power Density: 307.5 mW/m. Specific Power Output: 15.37 mW/g, enabled by an ultra-low density of 0.25 g/cm. Acoustic Sensing Range: 40 dB to 120 dB. Voltage Response: 1.05 V to 4.85 V across its sensing range. Mechanism: Utilizes a contact-and-separation mode TENG activated by acoustic-induced mobilization of a lightweight, flexible aerogel.

View Details Demo

Porous Materials and Structures for High-Performance Triboelectric Nanogenerators

Research analyzed diverse porous materials (hydrogels, aerogels, foams, textiles) across nano-to-macro scales for TENG dielectrics and electrodes, specifically developing an ultra-lightweight aerogel composite for sound-driven energy harvesting.

Technologies: Material Synthesis, Porous Composite, Sol-Gel Processing, Aerogel, Nanoparticle, Helmholtz Resonator, Self-Powered Sensing, Material Characterization (SEM, XRD, FTIR, DMA, WCA, TGA)

Results: Porous materials (aerogels, foams, textiles) enhance TENG performance and breathability for smart wearables and self-powered sensors.
